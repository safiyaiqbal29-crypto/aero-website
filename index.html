Aerodynamics of Wings

Aerodynamics is the branch of physics concerned with the interaction between air and moving bodies. Its principles govern how aircraft generate lift, overcome drag, and sustain controlled flight. Any object travelling through the atmosphere — from a rocket at launch to a kite in steady wind — is subject to aerodynamic forces.

The design of an aircraft’s wings plays a crucial role in determining its lift, stability, and overall aerodynamic efficiency.

What is Lift?

Lift is the upward force generated when air flows over an aircraft wing. Pressure differences between the top and bottom of the wing create this force.

Wing Design and Airfoils

Thickness

The thickness of a wing affects structural strength, drag, and internal space. In the early 1900s Ludwig Prandtl and his research team built wind tunnels to test the effects of wing thickness. They found thick wings are stronger and good for low-speed aircraft, but they produce more drag at high speeds. Thin wings reduce drag and are better for high-speed aircraft, but have less internal space.

Camber

Camber describes how curved the wing is. Early engineers such as the Wright brothers tested different airfoils in wind tunnels. They found that a more curved upper surface increases lift by creating a larger pressure difference between the top and bottom of the wing. Higher camber therefore produces more lift at lower speeds.

Different Airfoil Shapes

Airfoils vary in camber, thickness, and edge shape. Common types include high-lift airfoils, laminar airfoils, and supersonic airfoils. High-lift airfoils are highly cambered and used on low-speed aircraft. Laminar airfoils reduce drag by maintaining smooth airflow. Supersonic airfoils are very thin to minimise shock waves at high speeds.

Types of Aircraft Wings

Swept Wings

Used on high-speed aircraft and fighter jets to reduce drag.

High Aspect Ratio Wings

Long wings used on gliders for maximum lift and efficiency.

Delta Wings

Triangular wings used on some supersonic aircraft.

Glossary

  • Lift – Upward aerodynamic force.
  • Drag – Force opposing motion through air.
  • Airfoil – Cross-sectional shape of a wing.
  • Aspect Ratio – Ratio of wing span to wing width.
